Formula-RL: Competitive Self-driving Cars

We aim to create an artificial kart racing experience where contestants will train their own reinforcement learning-based kart driving models and compete with each other.

Abstract

With the development of representation learning, the area of reinforcement learning has become a powerful learning framework now capable of learning complex policies in high dimensional environments. We aim to create an artificial kart racing experience where contestants will train their own reinforcement learning-based kart driving models and compete with each other. The 3D racing track is designed and implemented by Unity3D platform with ML-Agents Toolkits. We have proved and compared the effectiveness of reward function and different components, including direction, speed, crash avoidance in kart racing games. Moreover, we have designed a new reward function that mitigate the bias of between human intuition and kart’s actual behaviors.

Our Experiment Settings and Results

Reward design impact hugely on how the model behaves. We incrementally tested how reward functions are impacting the model's behaviors, and did ablation studies to find out the most important factors in our reward design. We defined 4 objectives that we want the model to achieve:
1) Correctness: kart should pass checkpoints in the correct order,
2) Speed: kart should finish a lap as fast as possible,
3) Crash Avoidance: kart should reduce crashes as much as possible,
4) Direction: kart should always heading towards next checkpoint.
The details of experimentation results can be found in our report, the brief demo of 6 experiments is shown below:

No Reward

Since no reward is given, the kart will act randomly.

Correctness + Crash Avoidance

This reward design gives nearly the same bad performance as no reward. We speculate that since poorly-performed model will have low chance passing through checkpoints, the model barely gets reward during training.

Correctness + Crash Avoidance + Speed

This reward design also gives bad performance similar to no reward with the same reason.

Correctness + Crash Avoidance + Direction

This reward design improves the performance a lot since it guides the kart when it's driving between checkpoints. However, without speed reward, the kart drives in a slow speed.

Correctness + Crash Avoidance + Speed + Direction

This reward design achieves relatively good performance since it guides the kart between checkpoints and considered the speed factor. However, it collides more since the kart runs faster in the track.

Crash Avoidance + Improved Speed + Direction

This reward design achieves similarly good performance and the kart drives smoothly. More future works can be done to finetune this reward function.
